## nlin.SI

- The paper "Beyond descendants: integrable observables for cohomological field theories" explores new approaches to understanding cohomological field theories (CFTs). Here are some major themes and core concepts shared in this paper:
- **Cohomological Field Theories (CFTs)**: The paper delves into the realm of CFTs, which are a class of quantum field theories that have a rich mathematical structure. CFTs are used to describe the behavior of particles and fields in various areas of physics, including condensed matter physics and string theory.
- **Integrable Observables**: The authors introduce the concept of integrable observables, which are quantities that can be measured and computed exactly in a CFT. These observables are "integrable" in the sense that they can be expressed in terms of a finite number of parameters, making them more tractable and easier to analyze.
