### Bottom-up open EFT for non-Abelian gauge theory with dynamical color environment
- **Authors:** Yoshihiko Abe, Kanji Nishii
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22822v1

Here is a 3-sentence summary of the research abstract:

Researchers have developed a new open effective field theory (EFT) for non-Abelian gauge theories, which describes the interaction between a system and its environment in a local and gauge-covariant way. This framework retains environmental response variables and introduces a dynamical color-frame variable, allowing for nontrivial interactions and dissipation between the system and environment. The resulting theory provides a systematic description of color transport, memory effects, and fluctuation-dissipation structure in non-Abelian plasmas, and offers a new starting point for dissipative Yang-Mills EFTs with dynamic environments.

### How many systems can be dephased before the quantum switch becomes causally definite?
- **Authors:** Yassine Benhaj, Kuntal Sengupta, Cyril Branciard
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22807v1

Here is a summary of the research abstract in 3 concise sentences:

Researchers investigated how much nonclassicality is required for a quantum process to exhibit causal nonseparability, a property that can provide advantages over traditional quantum circuits. They found that dephasing all systems or only keeping the future system undephased results in a causally separable process, but retaining any other system undephased can preserve causal nonseparability. This behavior was observed in both bipartite and multipartite quantum processes, including those with quantum control, highlighting the robustness of causal nonseparability in certain scenarios.

### One-photon communication in atomic media
- **Authors:** Zixiang Hong, John C. Schotland
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22797v1

Researchers studied the transmission of single photons through an atomic medium, focusing on the loss of information that occurs during this process. They found that the fidelity of the quantum channel, which measures the accuracy of the transmission, decreases as the coupling strength between the photon and the medium increases. This discovery establishes a performance limit for quantum communication through various types of media, including deterministic and random channels.

### Geometric Origin of the Non-Adiabaticity Parameter and Self-Limiting Instability in Driven Nonlinear Systems
- **Authors:** A. M. Tishin
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22796v1

Here is a 3-sentence summary of the research abstract:

Researchers have found a geometric interpretation for the non-adiabaticity parameter, which represents the speed of a driven quantum state's evolution in projective Hilbert space. This framework allows for a local evaluation of non-adiabatic instability and its suppression at each stage of the evolution, providing a more precise understanding of the process. The study also identifies a nonlinear regulator that suppresses the evolution speed, leading to bounded dynamics and a compact criterion for self-limited non-adiabatic instability in driven bosonic systems.

- **Authors:** P. L. Krapivsky
- **Link:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.22383v1

Here is a 3-sentence summary of the research abstract:

This study examines aggregation-fragmentation processes where clusters can combine or break apart, with rates that may or may not depend on the cluster's mass. When fragmentation rates scale with mass, detailed balance only occurs when the scaling exponent is 1, but similar steady states are found for non-negative exponents using asymptotic techniques. For negative exponents, the system undergoes an instantaneous shattering transition, characterized by continuous mass loss.
