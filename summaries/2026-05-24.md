The integration of machine learning (ML) with traditional physics-based models has the potential to improve weather and climate prediction by combining the benefits of both approaches. This paper aims to provide a framework for blending ML and physics-based modeling systems, allowing for the identification of optimal approaches and routes to development and implementation. By combining the strengths of ML and physics-based systems, this framework supports informed decision-making and strategic planning for the transition to next-generation prediction systems.
